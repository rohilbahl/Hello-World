STAGES OF PHOTOSYNTHEIS

Light Reactions

Photosynthesis in the leaves of plants involves many steps, but it can be divided into two stages: the Light reactions and the Dark Reactions.
The light-dependent reactions take place in the thylakoid membrane and require a continuous supply of light energy. Chlorophylls absorb this light
energy, which is converted into chemical energy through the formation of two compounds, ATP, an energy storage molecule and NADPH reduced (electron bearing)
electron carrier. In this process, water molecules are also converted to oxygen gas, the oxygen we breathe!

Dark Reactions

The Dark Reactions(Calvin cycle), also called the light-independent reactions, takes place in the stroma and does not directly require light. Instead, the Calvin
cycle uses ATP and NADPH from the light dependent reactions to fix carbon dioxide and produce three carbon sugars, glyceraldehyde 3 phosphate, or G3P, molecules
which join up to form glucose.
